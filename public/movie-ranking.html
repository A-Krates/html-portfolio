My top 3 Movies

Passengers

Passengers (2016) is a visually stunning sci-fi romance film with a thought-provoking premise. Jennifer Lawrence and Chris Pratt star as two passengers on a long-haul spaceship to a new planet who wake up from their cryosleep capsules 90 years early. They must find a way to save the ship and its crew, while also grappling with the ethical implications of their situation. The film has been praised for its production values, performances, and special effects. However, it has also been criticized for its uneven plot and its portrayal of consent. Some viewers have found it difficult to forgive Pratt's character for waking Lawrence's character up without her consent. Overall, Passengers is a thought-provoking and entertaining film with a lot to offer. It is sure to stay with you long after the credits roll. Rating: 3.5 out of 5 stars

Black Mirror

Black Mirror is a British science fiction anthology television series created by Charlie Brooker. Episodes are stand-alone, usually set in an alternative present or the near future, often with a dark and satirical tone, though some are more experimental and less dystopian. The series explores modern society, particularly with regard to the unanticipated consequences of new technologies. Black Mirror has received critical acclaim for its writing, acting, directing, and thought-provoking themes. It has won numerous awards, including two British Academy Television Awards for Best Drama Series and two Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Television Movie. Here are some of the most popular episodes of Black Mirror: "15 Million Merits" (Season 1, Episode 2): In a dystopian future, people are forced to live on exercise bikes to generate power for the system. They can only earn credits by pedaling, which they can use to buy food, entertainment, and other necessities. One man, Bing, dreams of winning a talent show called Hot Shots so that he can escape his life on the bikes. "The Waldo Moment" (Season 2, Episode 3): A comedian is hired to perform as a talking blue bear named Waldo, but the character quickly becomes popular and is used to run for political office. "White Bear" (Season 2, Episode 2): A woman wakes up with amnesia in a house with a man who is being tortured by a group of masked people. She has no idea what is happening or who she is, but she must find a way to escape before it is too late. "Shut Up and Dance" (Season 3, Episode 3): A man is blackmailed into doing a series of increasingly dangerous tasks by a mysterious person who claims to have hacked into his computer and webcam. "San Junipero" (Season 3, Episode 4): In a futuristic world, two women fall in love in a virtual reality simulation called San Junipero. Black Mirror is a must-watch for fans of science fiction and thought-provoking television. It is a series that will stay with you long after you have finished watching it.

Better Than Us

Better Than Us (2018-2019) is a Russian science fiction television series created by Andrey Junkovsky and Aleksandr Vartanov. The series is set in a near-future Russia where advanced humanoid robots, known as "ARIs", are commonplace. The story follows Arisa, an ARI who escapes from a research facility and is taken in by a family. Arisa must learn to navigate the human world while also trying to evade the corporation that created her. Better Than Us has been praised for its production values, performances, and exploration of complex themes such as artificial intelligence, sentience, and the ethics of technology. The series has also been criticized for its slow pacing and some plot holes. Overall, Better Than Us is a thought-provoking and entertaining sci-fi series that is well worth watching. It is a series that will make you think about the future of technology and our relationship with artificial intelligence. Rating: 4 out of 5 stars If you are a fan of science fiction, thought-provoking television, or Russian dramas, then I highly recommend checking out Better Than Us. It is a unique and refreshing take on the AI genre.
